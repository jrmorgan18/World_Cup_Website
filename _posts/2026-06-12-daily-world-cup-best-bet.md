## Canada vs. Bosnia and Herzegovina - Under 2.5 Goals

<p style="display:inline-block;background:rgba(212,168,67,0.14);border:1px solid #d4a843;color:#d4a843;font-weight:700;padding:4px 14px;border-radius:6px;margin:0 0 10px;">Canada/Bosnia Under 2.5 Goals (-160 at DraftKings)</p>

Outside of the FanDuel special, the best angle on the board is in the early game between Canada and Bosnia and Herzegovina.

Canada are the rightful favorites playing at home, but I do not think this is a game that sets up for fireworks. Their recent form has been more solid than explosive, with just 11 goals scored across their last 10 games. They have enough talent to win this match, but they are not exactly rolling into the tournament as a free-flowing attacking machine. Add in the possibility that Alphonso Davies is limited or unavailable, and it becomes even harder to see Canada consistently pulling Bosnia apart.

Bosnia's recent scoring numbers look stronger on the surface, but there is some schedule context baked in there. Six goals against San Marino and four against Malta do a lot of heavy lifting. Against better competition, they have been much more likely to play tight, narrow games. Their last five matches all stayed under 2.5 goals, and that feels more representative of what this matchup should look like.

This is a World Cup opener for both teams, with Canada carrying the pressure of playing at home and Bosnia likely happy to make the game uncomfortable. Canada can win it, but I expect a tense, cautious match where one goal may be enough and two goals might be plenty.

If you do not like the -160 odds, consider parlaying the under with Canada double chance (win or draw), which gets the price closer to an even -110.
