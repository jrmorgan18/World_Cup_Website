Today's card is a little strange because there are very different incentives across the groups. Some teams are already through. Some are eliminated. Some are still chasing positioning, goal difference, or survival. That makes this less about blindly backing the better teams and more about finding the right game script.

## Ecuador vs. Germany - Under 2.75 Goals

<p style="display:inline-block;background:rgba(212,168,67,0.14);border:1px solid #d4a843;color:#d4a843;font-weight:700;padding:4px 14px;border-radius:6px;margin:0 0 10px;">Ecuador-Germany Under 2.75 Goals (+102.5 at Bet365)</p>

The first bet is Ecuador-Germany under 2.5 goals.

Germany have looked like an attacking machine on the scoreboard, but the underlying numbers tell a different story. They have scored nine goals through two matches, but the chance creation has not fully matched that output. That matters here, especially with Germany already through and likely more interested in managing minutes and staying sharp than turning this into a wide-open track meet.

Ecuador are the other half of the bet. They have an elite defense and have not scored themselves in the tournament. While there is some desperation here, that does not always equal goals. Ecuador's problem has not been effort or defensive structure. It has been turning possession and territory into actual end product. Against Germany, that is not suddenly an easy fix.

This feels like a game where Germany can control long stretches without needing to force anything. Ecuador may have to push at some point, but unless they find an early goal, the most likely scripts still look like 1-0, 2-0, or maybe 1-1. The market seems to be pricing Germany's nine goals more than the actual shape of this matchup.

## Netherlands -2.5 vs. Tunisia

<p style="display:inline-block;background:rgba(212,168,67,0.14);border:1px solid #d4a843;color:#d4a843;font-weight:700;padding:4px 14px;border-radius:6px;margin:0 0 10px;">Netherlands -2.5 Spread/Asian Line (+100 at Bet365)</p>

The second bet is Netherlands -2.5 against Tunisia.

This is an aggressive number, but the setup makes sense. Tunisia are eliminated and have been one of the weakest defensive teams in the group. They gave up five to Sweden and four to Japan, and now they have to face a Netherlands team that still has real incentive to keep pushing.

That is the key difference between this and some other heavy-favorite spots. The Netherlands are not just trying to get through the game. They are still battling with Japan for position at the top of the group, and goal difference and goals scored could matter. That gives them reason to keep attacking even if they get an early lead.

The Dutch already showed against Sweden how quickly they can pile up chances and goals once the game opens. Tunisia do not have much left to play for, and if they concede early, this could get ugly fast. The risk, obviously, is that the Netherlands win comfortably but only by two. But given Tunisia's form and the incentive for the Dutch to keep their foot down, I'm willing to take the bigger number.

## Japan vs. Sweden - Over 2.5 Goals

<p style="display:inline-block;background:rgba(212,168,67,0.14);border:1px solid #d4a843;color:#d4a843;font-weight:700;padding:4px 14px;border-radius:6px;margin:0 0 10px;">Japan-Sweden Over 2.5 Goals (-105 at DraftKings)</p>

The final bet is Japan-Sweden over 2.5 goals.

This has been the chaos group of the tournament, and I do not see a great reason to expect it to suddenly calm down. Japan drew the Netherlands 2-2, then beat Tunisia 4-0. Sweden beat Tunisia 5-1, then got hammered 5-1 by the Netherlands. There have been goals everywhere in this group.

The incentives also point toward an open game. Japan are not just trying to survive. They can still win the group. Sweden, meanwhile, need a result and cannot assume a passive approach will be enough. That matters because Sweden's best path is not sitting in a shell for 90 minutes. They have attacking talent, they have size, and they have enough forward quality to bother Japan.

Japan have been one of the best stories of the tournament so far, but they are also missing several key players, which can make the game a little more volatile. They are still cohesive and dangerous, but this does not profile like a slow, locked-down match. If Sweden score first, Japan have to chase. If Japan score first, Sweden have to open up. Either way, the game script gets us to chances.

This number is not sneaking up on anyone, but the matchup still points toward goals.
