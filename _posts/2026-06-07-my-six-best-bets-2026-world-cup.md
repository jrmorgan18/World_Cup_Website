Betting on soccer, especially a World Cup, can be fickle. A red card, a penalty, a goalkeeper standing on his head for 120 minutes, or one weird bounce off a defender's shin can swing a result and upend a tournament run.

So the goal here is not simply to pick the five teams I think are most likely to do something. It is to find the places where the odds do not quite match the actual path. In a 48-team tournament, that matters even more than usual. The bracket is bigger, more third-place teams survive, and a team's knockout draw can swing dramatically based on one result in the final group game.

Before getting to the picks, it's worth covering a few basic soccer betting quirks. The biggest difference is that most standard soccer bets have three possible outcomes: Team A wins, Team B wins, or the match ends in a draw. That is why you will often see a team listed as "to win" at a better price than you might expect. You are not just betting against the other team; you are also betting against the draw.

It is possible to avoid this by betting the "Asian handicaps" (basically the spread) or by betting the "Double Chance", where you get a team to win or draw, but the odds will be adjusted accordingly.

It is also important to know exactly what market you are betting. In soccer, many game-specific bets are graded only on the 90 minutes of regulation plus stoppage time unless the bet explicitly says "to advance," "to lift the trophy," or "including extra time and penalties." That distinction matters a lot in knockout games. A team can be the better side, survive extra time, win a penalty shootout and advance, while still not cashing a simple 90-minute moneyline bet. For the bets below, I am mostly looking at tournament futures, which are more straightforward, but it is always worth reading the fine print before placing any soccer bet.

Beware of individual player bets as well, as books will often list players to score or have shots on goal, who are popular names but may not even be in the starting lineups. So it's always good to check the announced lineups before any player bets. This could be pertinent in the final group games where teams already set to advance may rotate their squads and rest some key players.

With that in mind, here are my six favorite bets for the 2026 World Cup, in no particular order:

## Portugal to Win the World Cup

<p style="display:inline-block;background:rgba(212,168,67,0.14);border:1px solid #d4a843;color:#d4a843;font-weight:700;padding:4px 14px;border-radius:6px;margin:0 0 10px;">+900 at FanDuel</p>

Portugal came in at six in my rankings, but anyone among that top six is capable of winning the tournament. I think this is one of the most talented teams in the tournament with the ability to take down multiple top-end opponents in a row. They have arguably the best midfield in the tournament and talent throughout the roster, however they are still priced a full tier below Spain, France and England, and I'm not convinced that gap is justified.

They also have a top tier goalkeeper in Diogo Costa, which makes them dangerous in a knockout tournament. Some teams have one clear way to win. Portugal have several. They can control possession. They can play through midfield. They can attack quickly into space. They can generate chances from wide areas. They can bring real players off the bench instead of just names to fill out a roster. That matters in a tournament where teams are playing every few days and where the extra knockout round will test squad depth more than ever.

If Spain and France are the two best teams, Portugal is not far behind them. If England is being priced like a superpower based on talent and reputation, Portugal deserves to be in that conversation too. At this number, I'd rather take the deeper, more balanced team than chase a shorter favorite through a tournament that is almost guaranteed to get weird.

## Ecuador to Reach the Round of 16

<p style="display:inline-block;background:rgba(212,168,67,0.14);border:1px solid #d4a843;color:#d4a843;font-weight:700;padding:4px 14px;border-radius:6px;margin:0 0 10px;">+175 at Bet365</p>

This was a tough choice because Ecuador to win Group E at +400 is also tempting. Germany is the favorite, but this is not peak Germany. Ecuador are good enough defensively and athletic enough across the field to make that final group game uncomfortable.

Still, I prefer Ecuador to reach the Round of 16 at +175. It gives us more ways to win.

Ecuador were one of the best stories of South American qualifying. Finishing second in CONMEBOL behind Argentina is not some cute little accomplishment. That is a serious resume. More importantly, they did it with one of the best defensive records on the continent. In World Cup betting, that is exactly the kind of team I want to back at plus money. You do not need Ecuador to turn into Brazil. You need them to be organized, hard to break down, athletic in midfield, and clinical enough to take advantage of a few moments.

Morocco made a run to the semifinals in 2022 with a stiff defense and a capable counter-attack. Ecuador could easily replicate that formula.

Their group gives them a clear path. Germany is the biggest name, but they should have a clear edge over Ivory Coast and Curaçao should not trouble them. If Ecuador take care of business in the first two matches, they may enter the Germany game with a chance to win the group but without needing a result just to survive. And even if they finish second, the likely Round of 32 matchup is not some impossible heavyweight. The 2nd place path would probably match them up against Norway or Senegal, tough teams, but a matchup that either favors Ecuador or gives them even odds. If they win the group they would get a third place opponent who they would almost certainly be favored against.

There is also a soft factor that I think the market may be underrating. South American teams should feel a little more comfortable in this tournament than most European teams. The travel is easier, the fan bases should show up well in the United States, and the atmosphere for teams like Ecuador and Colombia may feel more like a home game than a neutral site.

At +175, Ecuador do not need to be a dark horse semifinal team. They just need to get through the group and win one winnable knockout game. For a team with that defensive profile, that price feels too generous.

## Japan to Win Group F

<p style="display:inline-block;background:rgba(212,168,67,0.14);border:1px solid #d4a843;color:#d4a843;font-weight:700;padding:4px 14px;border-radius:6px;margin:0 0 10px;">+300 at Bet365</p>

Japan are one of my favorite teams in the tournament from a pure soccer perspective, and this is the right way to bet them.

The safer play would be Japan to advance, but the payout is not nearly as interesting. The more aggressive play is to win Group F, where the market is still treating the Netherlands as the clear favorite. The Netherlands should be favored, but I do not think they should be treated like a team Japan cannot catch.

Japan have built on their 2022 World Cup breakthrough in a meaningful way. That run was not just a fluke where they happened to catch Germany and Spain napping. This is now a deep, tactically flexible, technically sound team with players scattered across high-level European leagues. They can press. They can sit deeper. They can shift shapes. They are comfortable without the ball but not helpless with it. That combination is extremely valuable in a group stage because they can adjust to the opponent instead of playing the same game three times and hoping it works.

That matters in Group F. The Netherlands have the most talent, but they are not unbeatable. Sweden snuck in the back door to the World Cup after a disastrous qualifying campaign. Tunisia are disciplined and annoying, but they will likely struggle to score enough to win the group. If Japan can get a result against the Dutch in the opener, this bet becomes live immediately.

The case here is that Japan are good in ways that tend to translate to tournament soccer. They are organized, experienced, adaptable and dangerous in transition. They also do not carry the same public betting tax as the bigger European names. The Netherlands are always going to attract respect because of the shirt, the history and the recognizable names. Japan are still too often treated like a fun underdog rather than a legitimate top-15ish team capable of winning a group.

At +300, I'll pay to find out if the market is still one tournament behind.

## Colombia to Reach the Quarterfinals

<p style="display:inline-block;background:rgba(212,168,67,0.14);border:1px solid #d4a843;color:#d4a843;font-weight:700;padding:4px 14px;border-radius:6px;margin:0 0 10px;">+280 at DraftKings</p>

Colombia to reach the Round of 16 at +110 is perfectly reasonable, but I'd rather take the bigger swing at +280 to reach the quarterfinals.

This is a bet on talent, form, and bracket leverage. Colombia are in Group K with Portugal, DR Congo and Uzbekistan. Portugal are the favorite, but Colombia have a real chance to steal the group. That is the difference between this being a decent bet and a very interesting one.

If Colombia are simply locked into second place, the path gets more complicated. But I do not think that should be the assumption. Colombia finished third in South American qualifying, reached the 2024 Copa América final, and have a star in Luis Díaz who can decide a knockout game by himself. They also have enough veteran structure around him to avoid looking like a one-man team.

The Portugal game in Miami is the swing point. On paper, Portugal have the deeper squad. In practice, that game could feel a lot more like a Colombia home game than a neutral-site match. If Colombia win the group, the whole bracket opens up. And even if they do not, they are talented enough to make life miserable for almost anyone in a one-off knockout game.

At +280, we are getting paid for the possibility that Colombia are not just "the other good team" in Portugal's group. They may be one of the ten best teams in the tournament, and if the bracket breaks even slightly in their favor, the quarterfinals are very much in play.

## USA to Reach the Round of 16

<p style="display:inline-block;background:rgba(212,168,67,0.14);border:1px solid #d4a843;color:#d4a843;font-weight:700;padding:4px 14px;border-radius:6px;margin:0 0 10px;">+125 at Bet365</p>

Ok, this may be somewhat of a homer pick, but I think it is actually a good value.

The U.S. do not need to make a miracle run for this to cash. They need to get out of the group, which they should, and then win one Round of 32 game, where they are likely to be favored or very close to it unless the bracket breaks in the worst possible way.

That is a very different bet than asking the U.S. to reach the quarterfinals or semifinals. Those bets require you to believe this team can beat a real heavyweight. This one mostly requires you to believe they can avoid disaster in the group and then beat a team from the next tier down. At home, with this roster, that is a reasonable ask.

The expanded format helps. The top two teams in each group advance, and eight third-place teams also go through. That means the U.S. have a lot of paths into the knockout rounds. Winning the group would be ideal, and they should have a real chance to do that. Finishing second would not be catastrophic, likely leading to a second round match against Egypt or Iran. Even third place might be enough depending on the results elsewhere.

The Chris Richards injury question is the obvious concern. He is their best center back and the U.S. defense is completely different without him. But this bet is not entirely dependent on Richards being 100 percent for the opener. The U.S. have enough talent and enough home-field advantage to get through this group, and the most important version of Richards is the one available for the knockout game.

The other value angle is simple line shopping. If one book is hanging plus money on the U.S. to reach the Round of 16 while other markets are treating them more like a favorite to get there (FanDuel has them at -130 to reach the round of 16), that is exactly the kind of discrepancy bettors should be looking for. Not every good bet needs a grand theory. Sometimes the number is just wrong.

At +125, the U.S. do not need to become a world power overnight. They just need to win the kind of game this generation is supposed to win at a home World Cup.

## Spain to Reach the Final

<p style="display:inline-block;background:rgba(212,168,67,0.14);border:1px solid #d4a843;color:#d4a843;font-weight:700;padding:4px 14px;border-radius:6px;margin:0 0 10px;">+245 at FanDuel</p>

I love Spain. They are my top team in the tournament, and if I had to pick one team to win the World Cup straight up, they would probably be the choice. They have the midfield control, the wide attackers, the young superstar, the tactical identity and the recent trophy to justify being treated like the best team in the world.

The reigning European champions check almost every box. They have tournament experience, midfield control, elite wide attackers, tactical identity and a manager who has already shown he can guide this group through a major competition. The only real concern is the bracket. Spain may have to beat another elite team before the final, and this is not a bet without risk. But every path to a World Cup final is going to require beating someone good.

At +245, I'm willing to back the team I think has the highest floor and maybe the highest ceiling in the tournament. If Spain play their game, they are good enough to make the whole bracket revolve around them.
