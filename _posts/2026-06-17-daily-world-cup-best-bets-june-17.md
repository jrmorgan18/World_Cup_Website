Another rough day with some late bad beats yesterday. Let's see if we can start to work back into the positive. Today's card has some big-name favorites, but the best bets are not necessarily the most obvious ones. Portugal should beat DR Congo. England have more talent than Croatia. Ghana may look like the better team on paper than Panama. But the sharper angles are more about game script than just picking winners.

## Panama +0.5 vs. Ghana

<p style="display:inline-block;background:rgba(212,168,67,0.14);border:1px solid #d4a843;color:#d4a843;font-weight:700;padding:4px 14px;border-radius:6px;margin:0 0 10px;">Panama +0.5 (-165 at DraftKings)</p>

The first bet is Panama +0.5 against Ghana.

This is not a bet that Panama are suddenly some powerhouse. It is a bet that this game is probably closer and uglier than the market suggests. Ghana have the bigger name and the more recognizable talent, but they also come into this match without a win in their last seven games. That is not exactly the profile of a team I want to trust as a favorite in a low-total World Cup opener.

Panama do not need to win this game for the bet to cash. They just need to get a result. In a game that should be tight, physical, and probably light on clear chances, the draw is very live. Ghana may still have the higher ceiling, but Panama are organized enough and experienced enough in CONCACAF chaos to make this uncomfortable.

With the half goal, we get the two best outcomes for the underdog: a Panama win or a draw. In a matchup where I do not see enough separation to justify Ghana being treated as the clear side, I will gladly take the goal.

For anyone looking for an alternate option, Panama +0.25 on the Asian handicap at -121 on Bet365 is also playable.

## England vs. Croatia - Under 2.5 Goals

<p style="display:inline-block;background:rgba(212,168,67,0.14);border:1px solid #d4a843;color:#d4a843;font-weight:700;padding:4px 14px;border-radius:6px;margin:0 0 10px;">Half unit: England-Croatia Under 2.5 Goals (-130 at DraftKings)</p>

<p style="display:inline-block;background:rgba(212,168,67,0.14);border:1px solid #d4a843;color:#d4a843;font-weight:700;padding:4px 14px;border-radius:6px;margin:0 0 10px;">Half unit: Both Teams to Score - No (-120 at DraftKings)</p>

The second bet is England-Croatia under 2.5 goals.

This is a matchup with plenty of attacking names, but the underlying profile points much more toward a controlled, cautious opener than a wide-open game. England and Croatia conceded just four goals combined across 16 qualifying matches, with England not allowing a single goal and Croatia conceding only four. That is not a fluke. Both teams are comfortable controlling games without turning them into track meets.

England should have more of the ball and more of the attacking territory, but Croatia are the kind of team that can slow the game down, clog the midfield, and make every possession feel like work. They have been doing this in major tournaments for years. They may not be the same peak Croatia team from 2018, but they still have enough experience and structure to keep this from becoming comfortable.

I like the under, but I also like the related angle of both teams to score - No. Rather than choosing one, I would split this into a half bet on each. The under gives us protection on 1-1, while BTTS No gives us protection on a 3-0 type England win. The shared core of both bets is the same: I do not expect both attacks to fully get loose.

## Portugal Win + Under 3.5 Goals vs. DR Congo

<p style="display:inline-block;background:rgba(212,168,67,0.14);border:1px solid #d4a843;color:#d4a843;font-weight:700;padding:4px 14px;border-radius:6px;margin:0 0 10px;">Portugal Win + Under 3.5 Goals (-110 at Bet365)</p>

The final bet is Portugal to win combined with under 3.5 goals.

Portugal are the better team and should win this match, but I do not want to pay the inflated moneyline or chase a big handicap just because the public sees Ronaldo and a heavy favorite. DR Congo are not a pushover. They are physical, organized, and good enough defensively to make Portugal work for their chances.

That makes the win plus under 3.5 a better way to play the favorite. Portugal can control the game, get their three points, and still not turn this into a blowout. A 1-0, 2-0, 2-1, or 3-0 win all cash the bet. That covers most of the realistic Portugal win scripts without needing them to run up the score.

This is the kind of World Cup opener where the favorite's class should eventually show, but the price on the obvious side is probably too rich. Instead of betting Portugal to win big, I would rather bet them to win professionally.
