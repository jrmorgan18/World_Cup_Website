## Scotland -1 Asian Handicap vs. Haiti

<p style="display:inline-block;background:rgba(212,168,67,0.14);border:1px solid #d4a843;color:#d4a843;font-weight:700;padding:4px 14px;border-radius:6px;margin:0 0 10px;">Scotland -1 Asian Handicap (-115 at Bet365)</p>

The first bet for today is Scotland -1 on the Asian handicap against Haiti.

This is a spot where I would rather back the better team to control the game than pay up for a moneyline that does not offer much value. Scotland are not exactly a team built to run up the score on everyone, but they should have a clear edge here in organization, physicality, midfield quality, and set pieces. Against Haiti, that may be enough.

The nice part about the -1 Asian handicap is that it gives us some protection if Scotland win but fail to fully pull away. A one-goal Scotland win is a push, while anything by two or more gets the bet home. That feels like the right way to attack this matchup. Haiti are a great story and should be competitive in stretches, but over 90 minutes Scotland should be able to wear them down and create enough chances to win comfortably.

## Türkiye Moneyline vs. Australia

<p style="display:inline-block;background:rgba(212,168,67,0.14);border:1px solid #d4a843;color:#d4a843;font-weight:700;padding:4px 14px;border-radius:6px;margin:0 0 10px;">Türkiye Moneyline (-145 at DraftKings)</p>

The second bet is Türkiye on the moneyline against Australia at -145.

Australia are usually not an easy team to play in this tournament. They are organized, physical, and comfortable making games ugly. They are rarely going to just hand you three points. But this Türkiye team has more high-end attacking talent and should have enough creativity to break through an Australian team that may spend long stretches defending.

The number is not exactly cheap, but it is still playable. Türkiye have the better roster, the more dangerous attacking pieces, and the higher ceiling. In a group where the U.S. and Paraguay already played their opener, Türkiye will know how valuable a win is here. They do not need to be spectacular; they just need to take care of business against a team they are better than.

For anyone looking for a slightly better price, Türkiye -0.75 on the Asian handicap at -115 is also worth considering. With that bet, a Türkiye win by one gives you a half-win, while a win by two or more cashes the full bet. I slightly prefer the safety of the moneyline, but the -0.75 is a good alternative if you want to squeeze out more value.
