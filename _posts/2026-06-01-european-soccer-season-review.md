## Champions League Final

The European club season came to a close in Budapest on Saturday, where Paris St. Germain once again found a way to finish the job on the biggest stage. The Champions League final was not quite the attacking showcase many expected, but it was a tense, high-level game between the two best teams in Europe. Arsenal struck early through Kai Havertz on a quick counter, PSG answered in the second half after Ousmane Dembélé converted a penalty won by Khvicha Kvaratskhelia, and the game eventually went to a shootout. PSG held their nerve, winning 4-3 on penalties after misses from Eberechi Eze and Gabriel, giving the French giants back-to-back Champions League titles.

It was a heartbreaking end for Arsenal, who had already completed a historic domestic season, winning their first Premier League title in 22 years. But for PSG, it confirmed what had been building all spring. The post-Mbappe project is no longer theoretical. Luis Enrique's team has become the best club team in Europe, built less around one superstar and more around synchronized pressing, midfield control, fullback aggression and a rotating group of attackers who all seem to fit the same idea.

**Raya** gets the nod after anchoring the best defense in England and backing it up with a standout Champions League run. He finished with 19 clean sheets in the Premier League and 9 in the Champions League, while winning the Premier League Golden Glove for the third straight season. Arsenal's defensive structure deserves plenty of credit, but Raya's command, shot stopping and calm distribution were essential to everything they did.

**Nuno Mendes** was the best left back in Europe this season and a perfect fit for PSG's suffocating, front-foot style. In Ligue 1, he led all fullbacks in combined goals and assists and finished third among fullbacks in big chances created. He carried that form into Europe as well, adding four goal contributions and creating eight big chances in the Champions League.

**Willian Pacho** was one of the defining defenders of PSG's season. He played a major role in their Champions League title run. His athleticism allowed Luis Enrique's team to defend aggressively, squeeze the field and take risks in possession without looking constantly vulnerable in transition.

**Gabriel** has become one of the best pure defenders in Europe. He was named to UEFA's Champions League Team of the Season and was a pillar of an Arsenal defense that finished with 19 clean sheets in all competitions. Gabriel is the tone setter, the physical presence and the player Arsenal most often relied on to win the first contact when games became chaotic.

**Vitinha** was the metronome of the best team in Europe and the player who made PSG's midfield feel inevitable. He was named to the official Champions League Team of the Season and then put in a fitting capstone performance in the final, winning Player of the Match as PSG beat Arsenal on penalties.

**Pedri** was a La Liga player of the year candidate and one of the cleanest midfielders in Europe. He finished with 2 goals and 12 assists in all competitions, including nine in La Liga. He is not just a pretty passer floating through games—he wins the ball, keeps it, advances it and makes Barcelona's attack run differently.

**Bruno Fernandes** put together one of the best creative seasons the Premier League has seen. He set a new Premier League single-season assists record with 21, added nine league goals and matched Kevin De Bruyne's record for most big chances created in a Premier League season with 33.

**Lamine Yamal** at 18 years old is simply one of the most unstoppable attackers in the world. He finished with 24 goals and 17 assists in all competitions, including 16 goals and 11 assists in La Liga. He led the league by far in big chances created and was second in successful dribbles per game.

**Khvicha Kvaratskhelia** is here because of the Champions League. He was named UEFA's Champions League Player of the Season after scoring 10 goals, adding six assists and winning the crucial penalty in the final. Nobody had a bigger European campaign.

**Michael Olise** finished with 22 goals and 26 assists in all competitions, hit the rare 15-goal, 15-assist mark in the Bundesliga, and tied for the Champions League assists lead with six. Bayern scored 122 Bundesliga goals, smashing the old league record, and Olise's combination of passing, dribbling and end product made him the creative hub of the attack.

## Premier League

Arsenal finally got over the line, winning their first Premier League title since 2004 and ending the long wait that has followed Mikel Arteta's rebuild. Manchester City pushed them, Manchester United surged to third after a turbulent start, and Aston Villa finished fourth while also winning the Europa League. Liverpool finished fifth, and because England secured an additional Champions League place through UEFA's performance spot, the top five qualified for next year's Champions League. Bournemouth and Sunderland were the surprise European qualifiers, while West Ham, Burnley and Wolves went down. Coventry and Ipswich earned automatic promotion from the Championship, while Hull City came up through the playoff.

## La Liga

Barcelona repeated as Spanish champions, winning their 29th league title and finishing ahead of Real Madrid. Villarreal and Atletico Madrid took the other Champions League places, with Real Betis also completing the top five. Real Sociedad earned a Europa League spot by winning the Copa del Rey, while Celta Vigo finished sixth and grabbed the other Europa League place. Getafe finished seventh and qualified for the Conference League. At the bottom, Real Oviedo, Girona and Mallorca were relegated, with Mallorca going down in brutal fashion on a head-to-head tiebreaker despite winning on the final day.

## Bundesliga

Bayern Munich returned to full domestic dominance, winning the Bundesliga and DFB-Pokal while putting up one of the most explosive attacking seasons in German soccer history. They scored 122 league goals, breaking the old Bundesliga record, with Kane and Olise forming the most productive attacking duo in Europe. Borussia Dortmund and RB Leipzig joined Bayern in the Champions League, while Stuttgart's fourth-place finish capped another excellent season. St. Pauli and Heidenheim were automatically relegated, and Wolfsburg joined them after losing the playoff to Paderborn.

## Serie A

Inter Milan reclaimed the Scudetto, winning their 21st Serie A title and completing a domestic double under Cristian Chivu. Napoli also returned to the Champions League, Roma returned to the Champions League as well, while Como produced one of the great stories of the European season by finishing fourth under Cesc Fàbregas. Milan finished fifth and Juventus sixth, leaving both Italian giants outside the Champions League at the same time for the first time in decades.

## Ligue 1

Paris St. Germain won their fifth straight Ligue 1 title and 14th overall, holding off a surprisingly stubborn challenge from Lens before beating them 2-0 late in the season to clinch the league. Lens finished second, secured Champions League football and then added the Coupe de France, making it one of the best seasons in club history. Nantes and Metz were relegated automatically, while Nice survived the relegation playoff against Saint-Étienne after a miserable season.

## Player of the Year

For my money, Harry Kane was the best player in Europe this season. PSG won the biggest trophy and Kvaratskhelia was the Champions League Player of the Season, so there is a very strong argument in that direction. Pedri had the best midfielder case, Yamal was the bright young star. But over the full campaign, nobody was more inevitable than Kane.

He finished with 61 goals across all competitions, including 36 in the Bundesliga and 14 in the Champions League, powering Bayern to another Bundesliga title and a historically explosive attacking season. He tied for the lead in Champions League combined goals and assists with Mbappé and Kvaratskhelia, and remained Bayern's reference point in possession. Kane was not just finishing moves. He dropped into midfield, linked with Olise and Luis Diaz, and then still arrived in the box as the most dangerous scorer in Europe.

That level of week-to-week production is difficult to argue against. A strong World Cup run with England could land him his first Ballon d'Or trophy.
